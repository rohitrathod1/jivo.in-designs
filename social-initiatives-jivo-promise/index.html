Company Structure

Mission-Driven Network

01

Structure

The structure is not a rigid hierarchy but a dynamic, mission-driven network.
It is designed to be a high-speed, self-correcting cooperation of individuals aligned toward a common goal.

02

Inspiration

It is inspired by the efficiency and accountability of a free market, where clear ownership and results drive success, not bureaucracy.

03

Alignment & Incentives

The primary incentive for all members is the shared "why"—a devotion to the mission of wellness and service (Sewa). The structure’s purpose is to remove all barriers to expressing this devotion. This mission-alignment is the critical incentive that vectors all individual efforts in the same direction.

04

Accountability

Each team, department, and individual has clear responsibilities, measurable performance indicators, and direct accountability for their outcomes. The organization is structured around the key "levers" (e.g., Product, Strategy, People), and each lever is owned by a team or individual responsible for its performance.

05

Communication

Communication is direct and transparent. All members are encouraged to "go direct to the source" to solve problems and find the root truth, bypassing bureaucratic layers. Speed and clarity are prioritized.

Goal & Responsibilities

Serve With Ownership

Goal

The goal is a structure where every person is empowered to act as an owner, fully accountable for their "micro-levers" (e.g., product, price, marketing) and fully aligned with the organization’s macro-goal: to serve.

Responsibilities

As an expression of these principles, the organization is responsible for:Offering the very best products and services that contribute to wellness.Being in service to all of humanity through its work.
Devoting its earnings back into this same purpose of service and wellness.

Policy

Policies are not a set of rigid rules, but simple, clear guidelines derived directly from our Operating Principles. Their purpose is to enable mission-focused action and speed, not to create bureaucracy.

Policy of Mission-First

If a policy and the mission ever conflict, the mission comes first. This requires an honest discussion about why the policy is failing, guided by integrity.
